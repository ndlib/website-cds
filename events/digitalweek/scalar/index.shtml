Workshop: Intro to SCALAR Publishing Platform

Register
Monday, October 6, 2 PM - 4 PM
Hesburgh Library, CDS Classroom

This workshop will serve as an introduction to SCALAR, a free, open-source authoring and publishing platform designed for scholars writing media-rich, long-form, born-digital scholarship. Developed by the Alliance for Networking Visual Culture at the University of Southern California, SCALAR allows scholars to assemble media from multiple sources and juxtapose that media with their own writing in a variety of ways; to annotate video, audio, images, source code, and text using the platform's build-in media annotation tools; and to structure essay- and book-length works in ways that take advantage of the unique capabilities of digital writing, including nested, recursive, and non-linear formats. This workshop will cover basic features of the platform, including a review of existing SCALAR books and a hands-on introduction to paths, tags, annotations, and importing media, and then move on to more advanced topics including the effective use of visualizations, annoting with media, and a primer on customizing appearances in SCALAR.

Keynote: Designing Digital Scholarship

Monday, October 6, 5 PM
Hesburgh Library Carey Auditorium
Reception to follow in the CDS

Tara McPherson is Associate Professor of Critical Studies at the University of Southern California's School of Cinematic Arts. In this talk, McPherson will ask, "What role might humanities scholarship play in the era of big data and networked computing? What special contributions can the humanities make that might help us understand - and design - technology differently?" In answering these questions, she will look at the work her USC team has undertaken over the last decade, including the digital journal, Vectors, and the new multimodal authoring platform, SCALAR. Their research has always been in direct dialogue with key issues in the interpretive humanities, including discussions of race, gender, sexuality, social justice, and power. Can such a dialogue come to shape the practice of software design?

Roundtable: Scholarship in the New Knowledge Economy

Tuesday, October 7, 2 PM - 4 PM
Hesburgh Library Rare Books and Special Collections

Digital advances are fundamentally altering traditional processes of scholarly communication. In this roundtable, speakers will discuss the significance of the shift from print to digital technology as the primary medium in which scholarship occurs.

Featuring: TaraMcPherson (USC), Erin McLaughlin (Writing & Rhetoric, Notre Dame), Elliot Visconsi (Office of Digital Learning, English, Law, Notre Dame), Jim Collins (FTT, Notre Dame), Tim Flanagan (Law, Notre Dame), John Wang (Hesburgh Libraries, Notre Dame)
