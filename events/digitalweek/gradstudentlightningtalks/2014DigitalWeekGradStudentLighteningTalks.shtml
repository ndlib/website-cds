New Technologies, New Knowledge

Graduate Student Lightning Talk Event


Application | Event Flyer

Thursday, October 9
9:30 AM - 12 PM
Hesburgh Library Center for Digital Scholarship (1st Floor Northeast)
All students, faculty, and staff welcome to attend. Coffee and pastries provided.

This inaugural "best of" digital scholarhip showcase will be comprised of 5-minute talks that allow graduate students from across campus departments to present their current research.

The theme of the event is "New Technologies, New Knowledge," and will focus on new digital technologies and/or computational approaches to research. Participants may present their work in the form of a focused summation, a concentrated case study, or a methodological exegesis. Presentations will be divided into thematic groups with question and discussion following each.


Application Information

Submissions are welcome on any topic relating to Digital Week event themes (anything from interface design and data analytics to intellectual property in the digital age). Preference will be given to advanced PhD students, but all graduate students are encouraged to apply. Applications will be selected according to the session theme, innovative nature of the research, and presentation clarity.
