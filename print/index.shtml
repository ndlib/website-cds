    Who Can Print 3D Objects

    Currently enrolled students and faculty/staff


    [top]

    Production Timeframe

    Patrons are strongly advised to submit their files at least 5 business days in advance of important deadlines. This becomes especially important at the end of the semester and during peak finals times.

    • Production timeframe may vary depending upon such factors as the object's printer file itself, staff and device availability, and/or unexpected device or material issues that may arise. Thus, the CDS does not guarantee specific completion times.

    • Generally, objects are printed on a first-come, first-served basis; however, priority will be given to students printing objects related to graded assignments for a course in which they are currently enrolled.


    [top]

    Specifications

    Supported File Types

    • .stl
    • .obj

    Maximum Build Size

    280 x 280 x 150mm (11 x 11 x 18 in)

    [top]

    Cost

    Price per Gram: $0.05 for non-course prints. There is no charge for course-related prints.

    • Cost will be charged to your account upon completion of print job.
    • Failed prints due to user error will still be charged to the patron's account.
    • Failed prints due to staff error or hardware failure will not be charged to the user's account.
    • The cost to print objects that have not been retrieved within two weeks of completion will be charged to the patron's account as a library fine.


    [top]

    Referrals

    The Hesburgh Libraries maintains an inventory of six 3D printers, with various capabilities. If we are not able to meet your needs within these capabilities, patrons are encouraged to contact the IDEA Center at Innovation Park. If we proactively feel that your project would benefit from a consultation with the IDEA Center, we will suggest that to you, and connect you with them.


    [top]

    Limitations
    • All patrons must comply with the University of Notre Dame Policy on the Responsible Use of Information Technology Resources (Policy 7.4). Examples of content that will not be produced includes, but is not limited to, the following:

      • Objects are in any way illegal, in violation of University rules/policies, or in violation of any applicable contracts/licenses (Policy Statement 2.1)
      • Objects that are part of production print jobs (large numbers of identical objects), as opposed to rapid prototypes (Policy Statement 2.3)
      • Objects that will be utilized for commercial purposes or for personal financial gain (2.4)
      • Objects that are obscene/offensive (except for officially approved, legitimate academic or University purposes) (Policy Statement 2.5)
      • Objects that infringe upon intellectual property rights (Policy Statement 2.6)

    • The CDS reserves the right to request written verification of University approval or assignment specifications from patrons, and/or to refuse production of content.
    • The CDS is not responsible for the functionality or quality of objects produced on the 3D printer.

    Who Can Print Large-Format Material

    Currently enrolled students and faculty/staff


    [top]

    Production Timeframe

    Patrons are strongly advised to submit their files at least 5 business days in advance of important deadlines.

    • Production timeframe may vary depending upon such factors as the file itself, staff and device availability, and/or unexpected device or material issues that may arise. Thus, the CDS does not guarantee specific completion times.

    • Generally, objects are printed on a first-come, first-served basis; however, priority will be given to students printing material related to graded assignments for a course in which they are currently enrolled, as well as students printing posters for conference presentations.


    [top]

    Specifications

    Supported File Types:

    • .pdf
    • .jpeg

    Image Size and Resolution: Files are printed exactly as received - we are not able to resize files.
    i.e., if a patron wishes to print a file at 14 in x 14 in, the file submitted must be 14 in x 14 in.

    • Tip: Use Google Advanced Image search to locate files that are a particular size/shape

      • Image size: at least larger than 1024 x 768

        • to determine a minimum size for a specific set of dimensions, multiply the largest dimension by 250
          e.g., 12 in x 18 in: 18 in x 250 = 4,500 minimum pixels

        • in the example above, try searching with an image size of "larger than 4 MP" or larger than 6 MP"

      • Aspect ratio: use this field to search for specialty shapes, such as square or panoramic

    For more information about selecting and preparing large format images to print, please contact the Visual Resources Center.

    [top]

    Cost

    Price per Square Foot: $3.50

      • Cost will be charged the patron's account upon completion of the print job.
        • e.g., 3 x 4 ft. poster = $42.00
      • Failed prints due to user error will still be charged to the patron's account.
      • Failed prints due to staff error or hardware failure will not be charged to the user's account.
      • The cost to print materials that have not been retrieved within two weeks of completion will be charged to the patron's account as a library fine.


    [top]

    Limitations
      • All patrons must comply with the University of Notre Dame Policy on the Responsible Use of Information Technology Resources (Policy 7.4).
      • The CDS reserves the right to request written verification of University approval or assignment specifications from patrons, and/or to refuse production of content.
